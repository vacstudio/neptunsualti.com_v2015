7. IDC / IE Quality Assurance
To ensure quality control for Instructor development each IDC and each IE must be declared to the HQ in advance.
The HQ need to have the possibility to check the course by an examiner.
ProTec Examiner is HQ staff member with special contracts. Examiner is qualified and ordered by the HQ to check IDC  done by IT and IE done by CD, just for quality assurance. Examiner is also certified to do any IT or CD work.
Course declaration in general:
ProTec demand for course declaration the course advertisement as an outline, to assure transparency and professional appearance of ProTec trainers. It is the same information, which the trainer should provide to customers.
This outline must include:
  • Course name and short description
  • Course schedule, date & time
  • Theory chapters listed
  • Practise exercises listed
  • Course terms & conditions
  • Price and what is included or not
  • Course location(s)
  • What if student fails
IDC Course concept verification
The IT has to send a copy of his/her course outline(s) by email to HQ - either as a .PDF or a link (to his/her course info Internet page) for auditing.
No reply from HQ is needed.
IE Course concept verification
The CD has to send a copy for each type of his/her course outline(s) by email to HQ, one time for every (new) version - either as a .PDF or a link (to his/her course info Internet page) for auditing/verification.
IE Course declaration
The CD must announce / declare the IE in advance online at the members area. Otherwise, the IE certification process might fail.
EXCEPTION case CROSSOVER
For Instructor crossover IDC / IE may be announced on same day just before certification.
Crossover means the Instructor candidate already hold same(!) level(s) form other organisation and join ProTec at same or lower level (no upgrade).
