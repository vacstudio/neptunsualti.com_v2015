Safety during practice
The maximum amount of participants in a practice exercise for instructors – for all standards. Generally speaking, the number of participants per instructor depends on available resources. The number of people for theory lessons is given by the amount of participants who can participate without quality restrictions of the course (e.g.: available teaching material or size of classroom).
The practical approach in the water is a different one.
Instructors must always be able to help a participant having problems without letting the others out of sight. As this is only possible if only one person has a problem, only one person at a time should exercise an assignment while the others observe to minimize the danger of someone else getting in trouble at same time.
Exercises in a pool can be executed by up to 6 participants at a time due to excellent visibility, negligible depth, short distances and therefore short rescue distances for the instructor.
However, in a lake or the sea the course participants have to be under permanent observation by the instructor, even if an emergency occurs and the instructor has to surface.
An instructor should not teach more than 2 people at once under water, one for each hand. To optimize the amount of participants per course, the help of assistants is recommended.
ProTec Standard for all excercises, water conditions and participants:
1 instructor -> 2 participants
1 instructor + 1 assistant -> 4-5 participants
1 instructor + 1 assistant + 1 rescue diver -> 6-8 participants
1 instructor + 1 assistant + 2 rescue divers -> 8-10 participants
Otherwise Murphy's Law is benefited!
