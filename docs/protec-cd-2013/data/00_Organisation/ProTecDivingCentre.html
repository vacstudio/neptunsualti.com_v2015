ProTec Diving Centre
We distinguish between three different types of ProTec diving centres:
On top the
ProTec Premier Centre / Premier School
To qualify as Premier School, a diving centre must offer nearly the complete ProTec course assortment.
This contains training courses for mixed gas (from Nitrox course to the Trimix course), cave diving or alternatively wreck diving, rebreather and instructor development by an IT/CD.
The retail offers breathing gas (fillings or rental air tanks with air, Nitrox, Trimix) and professional technical equipment.
ProTec Facility:
A ProTec Facility’s offer from basic scuba course up to Nitrox at least and rent Nitrox / air tanks or sell gas. Additional courses (e.g. cave or wreck diving) are welcomed.
In addition to general equipment and training, technical gear and literature are required.
ProTec School
A ProTec School’s offer from basic scuba course up to Nitrox at least, including manuals and technical literature.
Every registration requires a profile.
It must contain a description of:
  • foundation
    • principal purpose
    • size/complexity/catchment area
    • filling facilities for compressed air, Nitrox & Trimix (with photos)
    • diving school (photos of the class room)
    • offer of technical gear (photos of the shop)
Logistical requirements (e.g.: Nitrox/Trimix filling facility, Instructor- registration) have to be proved during 3 months.
Professional Technical Diving Instructor:
A ProTec instructor has to be a member of staff of a ProTec centre to be able to certify.
This should prevent dumping prices, ensure training quality, resources and protect the catchment area of the dive centre.
Time-to-time instructors are not favoured.
Our instructors should gather a certain minimum number of trainees to guarantee them a qualified training and to generate good earnings for themselves.
