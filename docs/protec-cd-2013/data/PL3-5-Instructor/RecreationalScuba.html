Recreational Scuba
“Basics“
Career:
1.) The instructor candidate (starting from PL-1) first has to learn course progress as assistant (- candidate) and the standards of the ProTec course types (e.g. Scuba Diver) which can be supervised as instructor later on. They may do this with every ProTec Instructor (starting from PL-3) entitled for it. Certification as Assistant Instructor by a PL-5 finishes this step. Crossover candidates must also know exactly the ProTec specific operational sequences and standards.
2.) As certified Assistant Instructor (PL-2) is taught special knowledge of Instructors (problems, safety, course organization/planning & execution, legal considerations, insurance) by a ProTec Instructor coach either during a season as Assistant or in a 3 to 4 weeks intensive course for instructors.
3.) As soon as the instructor candidate has acquired the necessary knowledge (crossover candidates of other agencies and/or during an upgrade to higher level (e.g. of 3 to 4 or 5) likewise) he finishes his training for the ProTec Instructor with an exam by a ProTec Course Director or, if possible, HQ can replace this by an internal exam of qualifications & references (e.g. by ProTec Instructor coach).
General for ProTec  INSTRUCTOR DEVELOPMENT
All pre-stage or basic Scuba diver education up to Scuba Diver 1 is done by ProTec SCUBA Instructor (I,IN), or higher level, see ProTec authorisation concept. This matters, that at each Scuba Instructor development and evaluation all the (included) lower level skills have to be checked also.
In fact, the whole skills of Basic (former Resort) Diver, Apprentice Diver & Junior Scuba Diver course are already part of the Scuba Diver 1 course. The Apnoea 1 (ABC) Diver skills have to be checked for each diver on the beginning of a basic Scuba course. For Instructor development, it is very important, that the Instructor candidate knows in detail how much knowledge and skills are part of all the different “basic” courses and how much time is needed for the course. As the Scuba Instructor must have all Scuba Diver 3 skills, Scuba Instructor is also allowed to certify “easy” Specialty Level Night Diver and Navigation like a Specialty Instructor, skills has to be checked at IDC/IE.
The next Instructor level, ProTec Advanced Scuba Instructor (AI) is qualified for Scuba Diver 2 (SD2) and Scuba Diver 3 (SD3). All the necessary minimum Specialty Instructor level for Night, Navigation, Search & Recovery, Rescue, Deep are included. For each specialty level a diver certificate is demanded. For evaluation upgrade to AI it is ok to examine a Scuba Instructor (I) for SD2, SD3 and all specialty skills listed above and – if well done – upgrade him/her direct to an AI without demand of existing specialty Instructor brevets. The AI candidate must also prove skills to run Specialty Courses Eqmt. Maint., Solo Diver, Wreck OR - alternatively, Cavern is recommended.

Scuba Instructor (PL-3)
General Requirements for
PROTEC 1* INSTRUCTOR (SCUBA INSTRUCTOR) - education / training (IDC) & evaluation (IE)
This points below for Scuba Instructor development has to be added to the general administrative requirements as written on the pages before. Contents has to be checked by a CD during a IE.
Preparation and course requirements
 
  • ProTec Scuba Diver 3 or equivalent or higher
  • Minimum age 18 years.
  • Sufficient dive practise, minimum 150 various dives.
  • First Aid certificate, made max. 1 year ago.
  • Medical statement – ok for working as an Instructor UW.
  • Successfully finished ProTec IDC
Course & evaluation
 
  • Crossover version: The ProTec Instructor aspirant already is an Instructor – so the main point it to check the ProTec specific standards and skills. But nevertheless it is important to check and repeat the general Instructor skills like how to teach, how to solve problems, how to prepare and act in case of emergency.
  • For every Instructor level the examination skills has to be much more substantial as for the same diver level.
  • The ProTec Instructor aspirant has to work out a course concept with timetable for each course. It has to show the course quantity, the effectiveness and if all ProTec standards are included. While evaluation, the aspirant has to be able to set up and go on at every point of every course (which are matter of this Instructor course) with his lecture at high quality. Contents, rhetoric, appearance and efficiency have to be checked also.
  • As an add on to the course level skills the aspirant has to be touched for safety and insurance matters, also legal matters. Further all ProTec internal like ProTec - Members Area Info, how to handle registration/certification and more.
  • For UW practise: The most important and difficult course parts has to be checked and simulated in a „complicated dive-student“ version, panic student, rescue exercise. IT simulate student. In general, every ProTec Instructor has to have full control over the whole course under water and be able to engage every time. In no case the Instructor is allowed to set him/herself out of acting in open water environment (like showing gear of and on UW to students). Only one student should demonstrate exercises at the same time. Others have to watch only. Use assistant Instructor as a helping hand. Rescue diver for safety backup.
Add one’s for every ProTec Instructor evaluation – paperwork sent to the HQ
 
  • The Instructor’s (aspirant) course concept(s) – see point 3, course & evaluation
  • The Instructor trainer’s course and evaluation protocol for IDC.
  • The course director’s (or examiner) examination protocol for IE.
 
Detail Requirements for
SCUBA INSTRUCTOR - education / training & evaluation
This points below for Scuba Instructor development has to be added & merged to the general requirements as written on the pages before.
Apnoea skills/exercises
  • 60 seconds stationary apnoea diving
  • 45 meter distance apnoea diving
  • 8 msw deep apnoea diving
  • Descending to 5 msw – applying a bowline knot there
Pool or close to shore/beach skills
  • 600 meter snorkel distance with complete scuba gear
  • Stationary floating at 3 msw. The candidate have 1 minute to adjust his/her buoyancy at about 3 msw. Then the candidate must keep stationary floating without descending or ascending more than 1 meter, without using any BCD/dry suit inflator or other help again, (dive gear preparation & buoyancy exercise)
  • The candidate has to perform pool exercises of Scuba diver 1 course as an Instructor, with briefing and debriefing. Especially „complicated dive-student“ must checked as well as the ability to see the red light in advance.
Open water skills (never develop dangerous situation - on purpose)
  • 20 to 30 msw depth. The candidate has to rescue / bring safety back to surface one diver who is in “passive panic mode” (just simulation). The whole dive group (students) has to be still under control while also ascending to surface. Especially the last few meters have to be done slowly. On surface, the diver has to be brought to land, show first aid
 
  • 10 msw depth. The candidate has to perform a Scuba diver 1 open water exercise with briefing, debriefing and has to watch out and avoid faults of standards or any developing dangerous situations. IT should simulate such situations in the role as SD1 student. The candidate must perform 100% ProTec standards.
 
  • 30 to 40 msw depth. The candidate has to prove good skills of orientation, navigation, leadership and problem solving, while guiding a dive group.  IT/CD has to check reactivity at depth and the ability to assess situations and solve (simulated) exercises.
 
  • 7 to 10 msw depth. The candidate has to gear off scuba equipment, ascent to surface, descent and gear on.
The certified ProTec Scuba Instructor is authorized to train and certify ProTec levels:
 
  • Apnoea 1 (ABC Diver)    
  • Junior Scuba Diver
  • Basic Scuba Diver (former Resort Diver)
  • SCUBA DIVER 1*  (CMAS P1)
  • Apprentice Diver
  • Speciality Night & Navigation

Detail Requirements for
ADVANCED SCUBA INSTRUCTOR - education / training & evaluation
This points below for Scuba Instructor development has to be added & merged to the general requirements as written on the pages before.
Theory checks / Exercises
  • Planning of a difficult dive with briefing and debriefing. The candidate has to draw a scratch of a dive site, plan the dive with all stops, and calculate air consumption, emergency procedures, and necessary UW communication/signs.
  • The candidate has to explain the difference between Scuba Diver 1 (SD1), SD2 and SD3 course in detail.
  • The candidate has to perform a theory lesson for a SD2
  • The candidate has to perform a theory lesson for a SD3
Apnoea skills
  • 60 seconds stationary apnoea diving
  • 45 meter distance apnoea diving
  • 8 msw deep apnoea diving
  • Descending to 5 msw – applying a bowline knot there
Pool or close to shore/beach skills
  • 600 meter snorkel distance with complete scuba gear
  • Stationary floating at 3 msw. The candidate have 1 minute to adjust his/her buoyancy at about 3 msw. Then the candidate must keep stationary floating without descending or ascending more than 1 meter, without using any BCD/dry suit inflator or other help again, (dive gear preparation & buoyancy exercise)
  • The candidate has to perform pool exercises of Scuba diver 2and SD3 course as an Instructor, with briefing and debriefing. Especially „complicated dive-student“ must checked as well as the ability to see the red light in advance.
Open water skills (never develop dangerous situation - on purpose)
  • The candidate has to perform 2 dive lessons about RESCUE as an Instructor.
  • The candidate has to perform 2 dive lessons about SD2 & SD3 as an Instructor.
  • The candidate has to perform a deco dive (max. 40 msw) as a dive leader, showing excellent dive control (over all – him/herself & the group)
  • 7 to 10 msw depth. The candidate has to gear off scuba equipment, ascent to surface, descent and gear on.
The certified ProTec Advanced Scuba Instructor is authorized to train and certify levels:
 
  • Apnoea 1 (ABC Diver)    
  • SCUBA DIVER 1* (CMAS P1)
  • Basic Scuba Diver (former Resort Diver)
  • SCUBA DIVER 2** (CMAS P2)
  • Apprentice Diver
  • SCUBA DIVER 3*** (CMAS P3)
  • Junior Scuba Diver
  • Speciality Night & Navigation
  • Speciality Rescue & Search & Recovery
  • Speciality Deep & Solo
  • Speciality Eqmt. Maint.
  • Speciality OHE (Wreck or Cavern)
