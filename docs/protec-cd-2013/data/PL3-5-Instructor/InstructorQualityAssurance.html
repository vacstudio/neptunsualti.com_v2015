4. Instructor Quality Assurance
For INSTRUCTOR CERTIFICATION, ProTec has a proved concept:
PREREQUISITES assures that the candidate is well prepared.
During an IDC (Instructor Development Course) the student learn all the skills and will be trained in theory and practise by an ProTec Instructor Trainer (IT).
At the IE (Instructor Evaluation) the student will be examined by an ProTec Course Director (CD).
The Instructor course CHECKLIST must be completed and signed by the CD.
4-eye Principe: No ProTec trainer may train / teach AND examine the same student for the same course level. So, regardless an CD might do IT work, For same course IT and CD must be two individuals.
A new instructor is asked to provide a course concept during an exam. What it meant? We generally differ between:

1.) Course standards - requirements and guidelines to be followed during course, a kind of quick reference. All requirements important for the course must be listed to account for the progress in training of the trainee and to enable comparisons with courses of other organizations. Production & publishing of ProTec course standards is matter of the HQ only.

2.) Course concept – lecture and workflow of a course.
Each topic should be mentioned including the (correct) solutions in note form.
A time schedule showing the amount of time assigned to each topic is requested.
A description of the location along with a dive profile applicable for the course and the regarded diving school is also expected. The selection of just one dive profile / place of course (if more than one possibility exists) is sufficient. The profile should outline individual exercise stops including depths, time lines with height above ground and ground condition (crushed stone, sand, mud, rock).
Naturally, concepts of different candidates differ by the personal style and in detail. As long as standards are fulfilled and the philosophy of the ProTec training is communicated, individuality is permitted.
A course concept represents not only exact guidance and documentation for a certain course of a specific diving school; it is also a quality assurance and thus a step towards European Union standardisation.
An CD and / or HQ should be able to determine the following on the basis of a course concept:
a) Are all ProTec standards included?
b) Is everything explained and understandable?
c) Are all practice exercises safe?
d) Can the concept be applied economically and technically?
e) Is the instructor candidate capable of planning a course correctly?
3.) Course manual - actually a record of the course content as it should be taught. This should help newcomers with their lecture style. In practice, printed manuals replace these records later on.

4.) Course declaration
ProTec recommend that all Instructors should have a course declaration to assure transparency and professional appearance of ProTec trainers. It is a course advertisement as an outline, It is the same information, which the trainer should provide to serious customers.
This outline have to include:
  • Course name and short description
  • Course schedule, date & time
  • Theory chapters listed
  • Practise exercises listed
  • Course terms & conditions
  • Price and what is included or not
  • Course location(s)
